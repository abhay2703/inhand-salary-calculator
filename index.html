    Understanding Your Salary

    How is In-Hand Salary Calculated?

    In-hand salary (or take-home salary) is the amount you receive in your bank account each month after all deductions. It is calculated by subtracting statutory deductions like Employee Provident Fund (EPF), Professional Tax, and Income Tax (TDS) from your gross salary. Your gross salary itself is derived from your CTC minus the employer's contributions.

    CTC vs Take-Home Salary

    Cost to Company (CTC) is the total expense your employer incurs for you annually. It includes your gross salary, employer PF contribution, gratuity, insurance, and any other benefits. Your take-home salary is always lower than CTC because of these employer-side costs and employee-side deductions. Typically, the in-hand salary ranges between 60% to 80% of CTC depending on salary structure and applicable taxes.

    Old Tax Regime vs New Tax Regime

    The New Tax Regime (default from FY 2023-24) offers lower tax rates and a higher basic exemption limit of ₹4,00,000, with a standard deduction of ₹75,000 and a rebate under Section 87A for income up to ₹12,00,000. However, it does not allow most exemptions and deductions like HRA, 80C, 80D, etc.

    The Old Tax Regime has higher tax rates but allows you to claim deductions under Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 lakh), HRA exemption, medical insurance (80D), home loan interest (Section 24), and others. If your total deductions are significant, the Old Regime may result in lower tax.

    For most salaried individuals with limited investments, the New Regime tends to be more beneficial. However, those with home loans, high HRA claims, and full 80C utilization may save more under the Old Regime.
